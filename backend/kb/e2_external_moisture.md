# E2 — External Moisture

Buildings must be constructed to prevent the penetration of water that could cause undue dampness, damage to building elements, or harm to people. Roofs and exterior walls must shed precipitation and manage surface water, and must not allow water to accumulate where it could cause damage. Concealed spaces and cavities are expected to be drained and ventilated where needed. A drained and vented cavity behind cladding is a common acceptable approach for weathertightness in many wall types.

Topics: weathertightness, cladding, cavity, flashing, roof, surface water, dampness.
