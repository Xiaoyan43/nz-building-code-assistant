# B2 — Durability

Building elements must remain functional for a minimum period appropriate to the building, the element's role, and how easily it can be accessed and replaced. Typical durability periods are 50 years for structural elements and elements that are difficult to access or replace, 15 years for elements that are moderately difficult to access or replace or where failure would go undetected, and 5 years for elements that are easy to access and replace and where failure would be obvious. Materials and assemblies must be selected so they meet the required period under the conditions they will face.

Topics: durability, service life, 50 years, 15 years, 5 years, materials, maintenance, replacement.
