# B1 — Structure

Buildings and their structural elements must withstand the combination of loads they are likely to experience — self-weight, imposted (live) loads, wind, snow, and earthquake — without failure or loss of amenity. Structures must have adequate stability, strength, and stiffness, and account for the site's conditions, including ground and soil behaviour. For light timber-framed houses within its scope and limits, NZS 3604 is a commonly used means of compliance; buildings outside those limits generally require specific engineering design.

Topics: structure, loads, wind, earthquake, seismic, stability, foundations, timber framing, NZS 3604.
