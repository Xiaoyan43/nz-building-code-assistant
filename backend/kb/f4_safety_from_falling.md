# F4 — Safety from Falling

Where people could fall a height that would cause injury, a barrier must be provided. Barriers are required on changes of level and around openings such as decks, balconies, stairs, landings, and the edges of floors. Barriers must be of a height and construction that prevents people from falling and resists the loads likely to be applied to them, and they must restrict the ability of young children to climb or pass through where appropriate. Common minimum barrier heights depend on the location (for example, around decks and balconies versus alongside stairs).
