# G12 — Water Supplies

Buildings must have a supply of potable water that is safe to drink and adequate for the building's use, protected from contamination. Hot and cold water must be delivered to the points where it is needed. Systems must guard against backflow that could contaminate the supply, and must control the risk of scalding — for example, water delivered to sanitary fixtures used for personal hygiene is limited to a safe temperature, with a lower maximum where vulnerable people are present.

Topics: water supply, potable water, hot water, cold water, backflow, scalding, tempering valve, sanitary fixtures.
