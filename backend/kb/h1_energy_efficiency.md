# H1 — Energy Efficiency

Buildings must be constructed to use energy efficiently, principally by limiting heat loss and gain through the building envelope and by making building services (such as hot water and HVAC) reasonably efficient. The thermal performance of the envelope — roof, walls, floors, and windows — is expected to meet minimum levels that vary by climate zone, with New Zealand divided into climate zones that set different insulation and glazing requirements. Recent updates raised the required thermal performance, particularly for windows and insulation.

Topics: energy efficiency, insulation, R-value, thermal envelope, glazing, windows, climate zones, heat loss, HVAC.
