# D1 — Access Routes

Buildings must have access routes that let people safely and reasonably enter, move within, and leave the building. Routes must suit the expected users, including, where required, people with disabilities, and must account for changes in level using stairs, ramps, or lifts as appropriate. Stairs and ramps must have safe gradients, dimensions, and slip-resistant surfaces, and handrails must be provided where needed. Accessible routes are required in many buildings to which the public has access or where people are employed.

Topics: access routes, accessibility, stairs, ramps, gradient, handrails, slip resistance, entry, egress, disability access.
